Hundred Islands National Park – Alaminos

One of the most iconic tourist destinations in Pangasinan is the breathtaking Hundred Islands National Park located in the city of Alaminos. This natural wonder is composed of more than one hundred limestone islands scattered across the clear waters of Lingayen Gulf. These islands were formed thousands of years ago through geological processes and coral growth, creating a unique seascape that attracts both local and international visitors.

Travelers who visit the Hundred Islands can experience a variety of exciting activities such as island hopping, snorkeling, kayaking, swimming, and cliff diving. Several islands such as Governor’s Island, Quezon Island, and Children’s Island have been developed for tourism and provide viewing decks, cottages, and scenic walkways for visitors.

Aside from its natural beauty, the park also plays an important role in the preservation of marine biodiversity in the region. The area is home to vibrant coral reefs and different species of fish and marine life, making it a valuable ecological destination as well as a popular tourist attraction.

Cape Bolinao Lighthouse

Standing proudly on a hill overlooking the vast West Philippine Sea, the Cape Bolinao Lighthouse is one of the tallest lighthouses in the Philippines and an important historical landmark in Pangasinan. Built during the American colonial period in 1905, the lighthouse served as a guiding light for ships navigating the coastal waters of northern Luzon.

The structure rises approximately 351 feet above sea level, allowing sailors to see its light from miles away. Today, the lighthouse remains operational and continues to serve as a navigational aid for maritime vessels passing through the area.

Aside from its historical significance, the lighthouse has become a popular tourist destination because of its stunning panoramic views. Visitors who climb the hill where the lighthouse stands can enjoy spectacular sunsets, ocean views, and the refreshing coastal breeze that makes the area truly memorable.

Balungao Hot Spring Resort

Located in the municipality of Balungao in eastern Pangasinan, the Balungao Hot Spring Resort is a well-known destination for relaxation and wellness tourism. The natural hot spring pools found in this resort are believed to contain mineral-rich water that can help relieve muscle tension and improve blood circulation.

The resort is surrounded by lush green trees and peaceful landscapes, providing visitors with a quiet escape from the busy environment of city life. Families and travelers often visit the resort to enjoy the warm therapeutic pools, picnic areas, and nearby eco-tourism attractions.

Balungao is also known for its eco-park which features a zipline ride and viewing deck overlooking the beautiful countryside of Pangasinan. Together, these attractions make Balungao a perfect destination for both adventure and relaxation.

The Cultural Identity of Pangasinan

Beyond its natural landscapes and tourist attractions, Pangasinan is also rich in cultural traditions and local heritage. The province is home to the Pangasinense people, who are known for their hospitality, strong community values, and vibrant festivals.

One of the most famous celebrations in the province is the Bangus Festival held annually in Dagupan City. This festival honors the province’s thriving milkfish industry and features colorful street dancing, culinary events, and the famous “Kalutan ed Dalan” where thousands of milkfish are grilled along the streets.

Local delicacies such as bagoong, tupig, and puto Calasiao are also important parts of Pangasinan’s cultural identity. These traditional foods represent the province’s culinary heritage and are enjoyed by both locals and visitors.

Through tourism and cultural preservation, Pangasinan continues to celebrate its history while sharing its traditions with the rest of the world.
